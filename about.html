About US

OUR Project

Seeds of change started in 2017 and we are currently registered with the Zambian Ministry of community development. Our focus is to increase the meaningful participation of young women and girls in Science, technology. Engineering and mathematics STEM. Zambia STEM program The Zambia STEM project aims to leveling up female representation in Science Technology Engineering and Mathematics fields by attracting, retaining, and advancing more girls and young women in STEM subjects and fields.

Our initiative emphasizes on computer science, programming and leadership as well as how Technology will be used to solve Africa’s economic, education and health problems in Africa, using integrated inquiry-based learning heavily based on hands-on learning experience. We seek to foster interest in STEM subjects to girls at an early age so that they are more engaged with science coursework. We give girls a chance to explore STEM disciplines while being introduced to technologies that use locally available resources to solve significant problems in their local communities.

Through this program we hope to increase the interest and choice of girls for SteM subjects by creating awareness on available career opportunities in STEM fields.

Our Core Values

Sustainability

Our strategies are planned to become self-sufficient and sustainable over time. We involve the teachers and school’s management in the planning leading up to all our events.


Transparency

Our strategies and impact school selection processes are unbiased. We pay attention to collate, utilize and report only true and correct reports.

Passion

The future is STEM, and through our strategies, we are passionately committed to preparing more girls for the future of work.
Our Objectives

Exposing the girls to the diverse aspects of STEM fields and the opportunities, mentorships, resources, scholarships and future training that they can harness to succeed in STEM.

To correct wrong notions and perceptions about girls in STEM and to break gender-based stereotypes and cultural barriers limiting girls from taking up subjects and careers in STEM

Attracting more girls to STEM by increasing the number of Junior Secondary School girls who choose STEM subjects and retain them through to tertiary levels, thereby increasing the female representation in STEM fields by 2040.

Mentoring and encouraging primary and secondary school girls who are yet to make decisions on their career choices and subjects to join STEM classes in senior secondary school.

Train girls and young women in coding skills and web development.

Reduce the cost of studying STEM in secondary school and tertiary education.

Empower teachers with modern pedagogical skills of teaching STEM subjects, for increased student engagement and learning.
